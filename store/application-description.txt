SHORT SUBTITLE (one line):
Durability and fatigue on one glance screen - power, heart rate and HRV.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

FatigueMeter turns your ride into one clear "keep going or ease off" read.

It is a full-screen data field that estimates how fatigue is building during a
ride, using three signals you already carry: mechanical power, heart rate, and
beat-to-beat RR intervals (HRV). No lab and no gas-exchange gear.

You flip to it a few times per ride when you want a decision - not another
number to stare at. It leads with a plain-language status:

  FRESH / PRODUCTIVE            You are riding within your aerobic means.
  FATIGUE BUILDING              Drift is accumulating; still productive work.
  DURABILITY MARKERS DRIFTING   The markers say the remaining work is now
                                mostly fatigue.

And because going deep into the red is often the whole point, it tells you WHY
you are there:

  Feat of Strength   The fatigue is being bought with real output - a big climb,
                     a breakaway, matches burned.
  Attrition          The fatigue is drift, with output fading. The hole-digging
                     kind.


WHAT IS ON THE SCREEN

  - A fatigue dial (AFI) with a green / amber / red band and a NOW marker.
  - An evidence row: DECOUP (aerobic decoupling), DFA-a1 (with a data-quality
    dot), kJ/ANCHOR (work done against your personal durability anchor), and
    MATCHES (W' depletions burned).
  - A feats strip: your best 1, 5 and 20 minute power for the ride.
  - A data-quality footer: RR artifact level, and whether DFA-a1 is live or the
    app has fallen back to decoupling-only.


IT LOGS EVERYTHING FOR LATER

Every marker is written into the FIT file as developer fields, so it flows to
Garmin Connect and third-party analysis tools. A per-ride session summary is
also kept on the device, so you can compare "was today a feat-of-strength day
or an attrition day?" across rides.

Note: FatigueMeter deliberately does NOT try to keep a chronic training-load
figure (CTL / ATL / form) on the watch. A data field only sees the rides it is
attached to, so it cannot do that honestly. It computes the load for THIS ride
and exports it; long-term load belongs in your training platform.


IT DEGRADES GRACEFULLY

Lose a sensor mid-ride and nothing else breaks. Drop the power meter and it
falls back to heart-rate based load. Drop the chest strap and it runs a
decoupling-only estimate. Any missing input greys out only the tiles that need
it. The screen never blanks and never crashes.


HONEST BY DESIGN

This is the part most fatigue apps skip. FatigueMeter separates what is
validated from what is inferred.

The validated backbone is standard, well-supported sports science: aerobic
decoupling, durability drift, and the population DFA-a1 = 0.75 aerobic
threshold anchor.

The fused fatigue index (AFI) and the durability advisory are an ESTIMATE, not
a measurement. There is no fatigue "ground truth" available on a bicycle, so
the index is calibrated to your own consistency, every screen carries a
persistent "advisory - not a validated measurement" tag, and the app never
issues a command - only a description.


TUNING IT

FatigueMeter is designed to be tuned to you. Nearly every threshold, band and
filter constant is exposed as a setting rather than hard-coded, and the
defaults are population values that will not fit everyone.

For a full guide to what each setting does, which ones actually matter, how to
calibrate the DFA-a1 threshold and the durability anchor to yourself, and the
evidence behind every number, see the project on GitHub:

  https://github.com/Macrophage87/FatigueMeter

Please read that before changing the advanced filter settings.


WHAT YOU NEED

  - A power meter - for decoupling, kJ, W' balance and matches.
  - Heart rate - for the fatigue index and heart-rate load.
  - An ANT+ RR-capable chest strap (Polar H10 class) - for DFA-a1 and HRV.
    FatigueMeter reads beat-to-beat RR directly from the ANT+ strap broadcast,
    so the strap must support ANT+ (a BLE-only strap will not work, and wrist
    optical heart rate is not accurate enough for DFA-a1). Without a usable RR
    source the app runs its decoupling-only fallback.

Add FatigueMeter to a full-screen data page, then set your FTP, critical power,
W', HR max/rest and sex in the app settings.


IMPORTANT

FatigueMeter is a training-analysis tool, not a medical device. It does not
diagnose overtraining syndrome or any medical condition. Its within-ride
fatigue estimate is not clinically validated, and the outdoor within-ride use
of DFA-a1 in particular is an area of active research. Use it as one input
alongside your own judgment and your coach.
